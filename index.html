Understanding the Problem Statement

1. Rapid Disease Spread & Public Health Risk

Kerala hosts millions of interstate migrant workers who live and work in high-density settings. Frequent movement between jobs, districts, and temporary settlements makes it extremely difficult to track infectious diseases like TB, vector-borne diseases, or viral outbreaks. Fragmented or absent health records delay case detection, hinder contact tracing, and increase the risk of disease spreading to both migrants and local communities.

2. Difficulty in Finding and Treating Migrants

Migrants often have scattered health data—paper slips, private clinics, employer lists, or nothing at all. When they relocate, doctors have no access to their medical history, leading to incomplete treatment, missed vaccinations, and duplication of tests. Language barriers, financial constraints, and temporary living conditions further prevent timely healthcare, making both detection and cure of infectious diseases harder, increasing public health vulnerability.

Project Objectives

Solution

Implement a Digital Health Record System for migrant workers with a Universal Digital Health ID (UDHI). Centralizes medical history including past illnesses, treatments, vaccinations, and lab reports. Authorized doctors access verified records instantly.

Uniqueness

Secure authentication ensures only authorized personnel can view sensitive health data. Each worker has individual health tracking; government dashboards provide analytics to monitor outbreaks and allocate resources.

Problem Solved

Migrant health records are often scattered or missing. Consolidates records enabling prompt identification of infectious or high-risk conditions, reducing public health risks.

Technologies Used

Frontend: Next.js with TypeScript and Tailwind CSS for a responsive, modern UI.

Backend: Node.js with Express.js and Firebase APIs for server logic and real-time data.

Security: JWT Authentication & Authorization for secure access.

App: Lightweight PWA for offline access and mobile performance.

Potential Impact & Benefits

Potential Impact

  • Empowers migrant workers with reliable digital health records.
  • Doctors access full medical histories instantly.
  • Real-time outbreak alerts help control diseases quickly.

Social Benefits

  • Equitable healthcare access for migrants.
  • Supports multilingual onboarding.
  • Maintains strict data privacy and security.

Environmental Benefits

  • Reduces paper usage and administrative load.
  • Minimizes physical documentation and travel.
  • Promotes sustainable health management practices.
